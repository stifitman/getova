This will be targeted at Staff members of Supporting People and Housing Services throughout Denbighshire.

Description of the contract

This is a time limited medium intensity floating support service for Service Users of both genders aged between 16 and 21. Service Users will be resident in Denbighshire and have been identified as being at risk of Child Sexual Exploitation (CSE).

The project should consist of a minimum of 6 units of floating support. Staff will deliver support activities to service users’ in their accommodation and in the community.

Support will be time limited and it is expected that Service Users will be supported up to a maximum of 6 months and after this time should have developed the skills to manage their safety around relationships and have secured accommodation appropriate to their needs. It is expected Service Users will be signposted to services who can work with the longer terms needs if appropriate.

Priority will be given to Service Users who are at risk, will be believed to be homeless and will be residing in Temporary Accommodation or will be sofa surfing. The Service can also be made available to Service Users engaging with Supporting People Providers who need support for CSE issues provided capacity of the service allows for this. Service Users will be identified as needing support to lower the risk of CSE and to obtain appropriate accommodation. The service will also be made available to those identified as needing pre-tenancy support in order to establish their own tenancy. This can be provided for a maximum 12 weeks.

The project will support a minimum of 6 people at any one time. Support worker hours will be managed dynamically and will be allocated flexibly in response to Service User need. Service User numbers will normally be expected to exceed the minimum level as new Service Users join the project while support is gradually withdrawn from those Service Users approaching the completion of their support. The project will be expected to have the capacity to accept urgent referrals as may be required from time to time.

Staff will deliver support activities in temporary accommodation, in service users’ own homes and in the community. Staff will additionally provide a 24 hour on-call service.

Support to obtain settled accommodation will be provided and shared accommodation should be considered if this is appropriate to service user’s needs, circumstances and choices.

The project will also be expected to provide a minimum of one education session per month for young people who are vulnerable to CSE and are currently engaging with Supporting People Services. Furthermore this service will provide 3 information sessions on identifying CSE and completing the SERAF (Sexual Exploitation Risk Assessment Framework) throughout the period of the contract. This will be targeted at Staff members of Supporting People and Housing Services throughout Denbighshire.
