Annex D1 General procurement

Justification for the award of the contract without prior publication of a contract notice in the Official Journal of the European Union (OJEU)
Directive 2004/18/EC

Part c) Technical Reasons and Reasons Connected with the Protection of Exclusive Rights: It is considered that this contract can be placed using the negotiated procedure without prior publication pursuant to Article 31(1)(b) of Directive 2004/18/EC (Regulation 32(2)(b)(ii and iii) of the UK Public Contracts Regulations 2015). This is because the services North Hertfordshire District Council requires must interface with existing Post Scanning and Indexing stored scanned post and associated indexes which are protected by Northgate Public Services (UK) Limited (Northgate) intellectual property rights. The existing software under this requirement is proprietary to Northgate and such additional services as this contract anticipates cannot be undertaken without access to these systems because the material on which it is based has been scanned and indexed into the system and destroyed. It is also considered that this contract can be placed using the negotiated procedure without prior publication because the system to be provided by Northgate contains functions that are difficult, if not impossible, for an alternative supplier to replicate in a single package. There are several suppliers who could provide individual aspects of the combined system but not with the interoperability that we require within the system or with our existing records and indexes. For these reasons North Hertfordshire District Council thinks it reasonable to award the contract to Northgate on the grounds that they are the only economic operator with the technical ability to provide the services North Hertfordshire District Council requires in an interconnecting system. Introducing a change of supplier will introduce material and different technical characteristics which will result in incompatibility .and significantly increased costs to NHDC, which would effectively preclude any other supplier from being able to make an economically successful bid in any event. The use of a VEAT Notice is permitted as full procurement for this contract would not be an efficient or cost effective use of the Councils resources and would be contrary to the Councils objectives including those to promote and develop external partnerships to meet strategic objectives.
